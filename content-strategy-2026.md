## 4. CONTENT FORMAT RECOMMENDATIONS

### What Works Best for Pierre's Audience

Based on the audit, competitor analysis, and the target audience (responsables pedagogiques, coordinateurs, directeurs d'ecoles privees, ingenieurs pedagogiques), here are the recommended content formats ranked by impact:

#### Tier 1 -- Highest Impact

**1. Practitioner Field Reports (REX -- Retour d'Experience)**
- Why: This is Pierre's strongest differentiator. The francophone education content space is dominated by theory (academic), vendor marketing (EdTech companies), and regulatory summaries (certification bodies). Almost nobody publishes "I did this, here is what happened."
- Examples: AI field report, multi-campus harmonization, LMS deployment story
- Expected performance: Highest LinkedIn engagement, strongest consulting conversion

**2. Practical How-To Guides with Checklists**
- Why: Captures transactional search intent ("how do I..."). Provides immediate value. Gets bookmarked and shared in professional networks.
- Examples: Qualiopi audit prep, RNCP competency blocks, rentree checklist
- Expected performance: Highest organic traffic, strong evergreen value

#### Tier 2 -- Strong Impact

**3. Decision Frameworks**
- Why: Helps readers make choices (which LMS, which AI tool, how to structure evaluations). Positions Pierre as the expert who has already navigated these decisions.
- Examples: LMS selection, training impact measurement, no-code tool choice
- Expected performance: Good organic traffic, strong consulting lead generation

**4. Opinion Pieces (Prise de Position)**
- Why: Establishes thought leadership. Pierre's anti-storytelling, direct tone is perfectly suited for opinion content that takes a clear stance.
- Examples: "PowerPoint training doesn't work", "Atypical paths are an asset", "Satisfaction surveys are not enough"
- Expected performance: Highest LinkedIn engagement, strong brand building

#### Tier 3 -- Supporting Content

**5. Seasonal/Timely Content**
- Why: Captures predictable traffic spikes and feels current. Gets shared during relevant periods.
- Examples: Rentree checklist (August), RNCP reform updates, Qualiopi changes
- Expected performance: Strong short-term traffic, good LinkedIn sharing

**6. Meta/Retrospective Content**
- Why: Transparency about process and results builds trust. Rare in the French professional blogging space.
- Examples: 9-month blogging retrospective, content creation lessons
- Expected performance: Good LinkedIn engagement, community building

### Formats to AVOID

- **Listicles without substance** ("10 outils EdTech a connaitre") -- too generic, already saturated by EdTech vendors
- **Academic/theoretical deep dives** -- not Pierre's voice, doesn't differentiate from university blogs
- **News aggregation** ("Ce qui change en 2026") -- better suited for LinkedIn posts via the Veille Hebdo format in the LinkedIn Studio

## 5. CROSS-PLATFORM STRATEGY: Blog <> LinkedIn Integration

Pierre has a unique advantage: the LinkedIn Studio already built into his admin panel, with specific workflows for blog-to-LinkedIn and LinkedIn-to-blog conversion. Here is how to maximize this.

### The Content Cascade Model

```
BLOG ARTICLE (long-form, SEO-optimized, permanent)
    |
    +--> LinkedIn Post 1: Key insight extraction (analyse format)
    |        Published day of blog article
    |
    +--> LinkedIn Post 2: Provocative angle from the article (opinion format)
    |        Published 3-4 days later
    |
    +--> LinkedIn Post 3: Practical takeaway / checklist excerpt (fait format)
    |        Published 7-10 days later
    |
    +--> LinkedIn Visual: Carousel summarizing key points
    |        Published alongside Post 1 or 2
    |
    +--> LinkedIn Newsletter: Monthly digest of 2 articles (newsletter format)
             Published end of month
```

### Blog --> LinkedIn Workflow (using existing "Depuis le blog" tab)

For each of the 12 planned articles:

1. **Day 0 (publication day)**: Use the "Decliner en post LinkedIn" button to generate an `analyse` format post. Lead with the most surprising insight from the article. Include a link to the full article. Generate a square (1200x1200) visual with the article title.

2. **Day 3-4**: Manually create an `opinion` format post that takes one controversial position from the article and expands it. NO link to the blog -- this post stands alone. The goal is engagement, not traffic.

3. **Day 7-10**: Create a `fait` format post with one concrete data point or practical tip from the article. Include a carousel (2-4 slides) summarizing the actionable steps.

4. **End of month**: Compile the 2 monthly articles into a `newsletter` format post. This becomes the monthly LinkedIn newsletter edition.

### LinkedIn --> Blog Pipeline (using existing "LinkedIn > Blog" tab)

Not every LinkedIn post should become a blog article, but certain signals indicate an article opportunity:

- **Performance signal**: Any LinkedIn post rated "carton" in the history metrics
- **Question signal**: Posts that generate many comments asking "how" or "can you expand on this"
- **Series signal**: When 3+ LinkedIn posts cluster around the same topic, they can be consolidated into a comprehensive blog article

**Recommended monthly cadence**:
- 2 blog articles (from the editorial calendar above)
- 4-6 LinkedIn posts derived from those articles
- 1-2 standalone LinkedIn posts from veille hebdo
- 0-1 LinkedIn-to-blog conversion (when a post overperforms)

### LinkedIn Banner Strategy

Use the 5-slide LinkedIn Premium banner slideshow to promote the current month's content pillar:

- Slide 1: Personal brand statement (permanent)
- Slide 2: Latest blog article title + CTA
- Slide 3: Key metric or quote from a recent article
- Slide 4: Consulting/speaking availability
- Slide 5: Gradly product highlight

Update slides 2 and 3 with each new article publication.

### Content Feedback Loop

The LinkedIn Studio's history tab with performance metrics (flop/correct/carton) should directly inform the editorial calendar:

- **Articles derived from "carton" LinkedIn topics** get priority in the calendar
- **Blog articles that generate low LinkedIn engagement** signal a topic to reframe or abandon
- **The calendrier editorial's "stale tag detection"** identifies topics Pierre hasn't covered recently -- these become candidates for the next planning cycle
- **The tone feedback system** (injected into Claude prompts) should evolve based on which LinkedIn voice resonates most

### Bilingual Expansion Strategy

Starting Month 3 (June 2026), begin publishing selected articles in English:

1. Choose the 1 article per month with the strongest international appeal (LMS selection, AI field report, competency-based assessment)
2. Use the admin CMS to generate an English version
3. Publish under `/en/blog/` with proper hreflang linking
4. Create a separate LinkedIn post in English targeting the international instructional design community

**Goal**: By September 2026, have 4-6 English articles establishing Pierre in the anglophone instructional design conversation.
