# Content & Copywriting Audit
## pierretouzet.fr -- FR & EN Portfolio
### Audited: March 20, 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Pierre Touzet's portfolio is a well-structured, bilingual site with a strong professional foundation. The voice is direct, practitioner-first, and refreshingly free of corporate platitudes. However, the content has several copywriting weak points that limit its conversion potential: the headline lacks specificity, the value proposition buries the lead, CTAs are generic, and the EN version has translation inconsistencies that weaken the messaging. The blog strategy is nascent but well-positioned.

**Overall Score: 6.8 / 10** -- Solid foundation, significant room for optimization.

---

## 1. HEADLINE EFFECTIVENESS -- Score: 5/10

### Current Headlines

**FR**: "Je crois que l'education peut changer les choses."
**EN**: "I believe education can change the world."

### Analysis

These headlines suffer from three problems:

**Problem 1: Belief is not a value proposition.** "Je crois que..." is a personal conviction, not a promise to the visitor. The visitor does not come to the site to learn what Pierre believes -- they come to learn what Pierre can do for them. Starting with "I believe" is passive. It positions Pierre as a thinker, not a doer.

**Problem 2: "Changer les choses" vs. "change the world" is a translation inconsistency.** The French says "change things" (modest, grounded). The English says "change the world" (grandiose, borderline cliche). This is not just a translation gap -- it creates two different positioning statements. The FR version is more authentic to Pierre's anti-storytelling voice. The EN version sounds like a TED Talk opener.

**Problem 3: The headline is generic.** Remove Pierre's name, and this headline could belong to any educator, edtech startup, or NGO. It says nothing about what makes Pierre unique: 15 years bridging IT and education, 6 campuses, a tool he built, his hands-on practitioner identity.

### Recommended Rewrites

**Option A -- Direct and specific (recommended)**
- FR: "Je rends le complexe accessible. 15 ans entre IT, pedagogie et innovation digitale."
- EN: "I make the complex accessible. 15 years across IT, education, and digital innovation."

Note: This takes the current subheadline opener and promotes it to headline status. The subheadline is stronger than the headline -- that is the problem.

**Option B -- Result-oriented**
- FR: "6 campus. 400 apprenants. Une obsession : simplifier ce qui est complexe."
- EN: "6 campuses. 400 learners. One obsession: making the complex simple."

**Option C -- Expert positioning (if targeting consulting/hiring leads)**
- FR: "Ingenieur pedagogique. 15 ans a transformer des organisations educatives."
- EN: "Instructional designer. 15 years transforming educational organizations."

**Why it matters**: The headline is the single highest-impact piece of copy on the site. It is the first thing visitors read, the text that appears in search results and social shares, and the primary factor in whether someone stays or bounces.

## 2. VALUE PROPOSITION CLARITY -- Score: 6/10

### Current Description

**FR**: "Alors je rends le complexe accessible. 15 ans entre IT, pedagogie et innovation digitale -- je pilote 6 campus nationaux, j'ai cree Gradly, et je vulgarise tout ce qui me tombe sous la main. Disponible en consulting et freelance."

**EN**: "So I make the complex accessible. 15 years across IT, education and digital innovation -- I manage 6 national campuses, built Gradly, and I break down anything I get my hands on. Available for consulting and freelance."

### Analysis

**Strength**: The description is well-structured. It follows a logical sequence: capability > experience > proof points > availability. The "15 years across IT, education and digital innovation" is the strongest clause -- it immediately communicates a rare cross-domain expertise.

**Weakness 1: "Alors" / "So" creates dependency on the headline.** The description only makes sense if you read the headline first. Since many visitors will scan directly to the description (especially on mobile), the "So" feels disconnected.

**Weakness 2: "Je vulgarise tout ce qui me tombe sous la main" / "I break down anything I get my hands on" is too casual.** While it conveys versatility, the phrasing undermines professional authority. A hiring manager or school director does not want to hear that you "break down anything you get your hands on" -- they want to hear that you solve their specific problem.

**Weakness 3: "Disponible en consulting et freelance" is buried.** This is the single most actionable piece of information for potential clients, yet it is tacked on as the last sentence. For a consulting-oriented portfolio, availability should be visually prominent, not a sentence fragment.

### Recommended Rewrite

**FR**: "15 ans entre IT, pedagogie et innovation digitale. Je pilote 6 campus nationaux, j'ai cree Gradly pour automatiser la gestion RNCP, et j'accompagne les organisations educatives dans leur transformation. Disponible en consulting et freelance."

**EN**: "15 years across IT, education, and digital innovation. I manage 6 national campuses, built Gradly to automate RNCP management, and help educational organizations navigate their transformation. Available for consulting and freelance."

Changes made:
- Removed the "So" / "Alors" dependency
- Replaced the casual "vulgarise tout ce qui me tombe sous la main" with a more specific statement about what Pierre does
- Added "pour automatiser la gestion RNCP" to give Gradly a concrete purpose (the current version just names it)
- "Accompagne les organisations educatives dans leur transformation" is more precise than "break down anything"

## 3. CALL-TO-ACTION QUALITY -- Score: 5/10

### Current CTAs

| Location | FR CTA | EN CTA |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Hero primary | Decouvrir mes projets | Discover my projects |
| Hero secondary | Me contacter | Contact me |
| Hero tertiary | Telecharger mon CV | Download my CV |
| Contact section | Parlons-en | Let's talk |
| Consulting page | Parlons-en | Let's talk |
| Hiring page | M'envoyer un email | Send me an email |
| Ebook | Telecharger gratuitement | Download for free |
| Speaker | M'inviter a intervenir | Invite me to speak |

### Analysis

**Problem 1: The primary CTA is low-intent.** "Discover my projects" sends visitors deeper into the portfolio instead of toward conversion. The primary CTA should be the action Pierre most wants visitors to take. If the goal is consulting leads, the primary CTA should be "Parlons de votre projet" / "Let's discuss your project." If the goal is demonstrating expertise, the projects CTA is fine -- but it should be secondary.

**Problem 2: "Me contacter" / "Contact me" is the most generic CTA possible.** It provides no context about what happens when someone contacts Pierre. A better CTA conveys the outcome: "Discutons de votre projet" / "Book a free consultation" / "Demander un diagnostic".

**Problem 3: "Parlons-en" / "Let's talk" on both consulting and contact pages is identical.** Each page should have a CTA tailored to its context. The consulting page should emphasize the mission outcome. The contact page can be more general.

**Problem 4: "M'envoyer un email" on the hiring page is too passive.** Someone ready to hire does not want to "send an email" -- they want to "explore a collaboration" or "discuss a role."

### Recommended CTA Improvements

| Location | Current FR | Recommended FR | Current EN | Recommended EN |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-----------|----------------|
| Hero primary | Decouvrir mes projets | Voir ce que je fais | Discover my projects | See what I do |
| Hero secondary | Me contacter | Discutons de votre projet | Contact me | Let's discuss your project |
| Consulting page | Parlons-en | Demander un diagnostic | Let's talk | Request a diagnostic |
| Hiring page | M'envoyer un email | Explorer une collaboration | Send me an email | Explore a collaboration |
| Contact subtitle | Besoin d'un regard expert... Parlons-en. | Besoin d'un regard expert... Ecrivez-moi. | Need expert insight... Let's talk. | Need expert insight... Reach out. |

## 4. CONTENT STRUCTURE & SCANABILITY -- Score: 8/10

### Analysis

This is the portfolio's strongest area. The content is well-organized with:

**Strengths:**
- Clear section hierarchy on the homepage (hero > metrics > projects > process > experience > skills > testimonials > CTAs)
- Mono-spaced labels and section numbers ("01", "02") create a clean information architecture
- Key metrics (15 / 6 / 400+ / 6+) are immediately visible and impactful
- The consulting page follows a logical sales funnel: services > process > metrics > use cases > formats > testimonials > CTA
- The 4-step process (Observer > Concevoir > Deployer > Iterer) is memorable and well-formatted
- Short paragraphs, no walls of text

**Minor issues:**
- The homepage has many sections but no clear narrative progression from "who is Pierre" to "what can he do for you" to "why should you trust him" to "what should you do next." The sections are individually good but the overall page feels like a catalog rather than a guided journey.
- The "Ils ont fait confiance" / "Trusted by" line lists company names (IEFT, IFAG, ICM, C&D) that mean nothing to visitors outside French private higher education. Consider adding a brief descriptor or replacing with industry-level social proof ("ecoles privees", "reseaux multi-campus").

## 5. PROFESSIONAL POSITIONING -- Score: 7/10

### Analysis

**Strengths:**
- The positioning at the intersection of IT, pedagogy, and digital innovation is genuinely distinctive. Very few professionals in France occupy this exact niche.
- The progression from IT technician to national program director tells a compelling story of capability expansion.
- Gradly serves as excellent proof of problem-solving ability.
- The speaker topics reinforce expertise without being self-promotional.
- The consulting page use cases ("Vous etes directeur d'ecole et vos programmes datent") are well-targeted.

**Weaknesses:**

**Weak positioning 1: Title inflation.** The EN version calls the current role "National Program Director" while the FR says "Responsable national des programmes." The EN title implies C-level authority. If Pierre's actual title is "Responsable," the EN should be "National Programs Manager" or "Head of National Programs" -- not "Director." Inflated titles are immediately spotted by recruiters and erode trust.

**Weak positioning 2: "Multipotentialiste assume."** While this is authentic, it can read as "I cannot focus on one thing." For a portfolio targeting institutional clients and employers, the multi-format content experience is better framed as "versatile communicator" or "multi-channel content strategist" rather than as a personality trait.

**Weak positioning 3: The ebook at 10 pages.** Calling a 10-page document an "ebook" sets low expectations. Consider reframing as "guide pratique" / "practical guide" or "livre blanc" / "white paper" which carry more professional weight in the French education market.

**Weak positioning 4: The "bac pro" is mentioned but not leveraged.** Pierre's educational background (baccalaureat professionnel in electrotechnics) is a significant differentiator in a field dominated by Master's degree holders. The current copy mentions it neutrally in the education section. It should be positioned as a strength more aggressively, especially in the speaker topics and the "hello" page -- this is a genuine anti-establishment story that resonates with the "parcours atypique" narrative.

## 6. TRANSLATION QUALITY (FR vs EN Consistency) -- Score: 6/10

### Systematic Issues

**Issue 1: Tone shift.** The FR version is confident-modest ("Je crois que l'education peut changer les choses" = things). The EN version is confident-grandiose ("I believe education can change the world" = the world). This pattern recurs throughout: the EN copy tends to amplify claims that the FR copy keeps grounded.

**Issue 2: Cultural adaptation is inconsistent.** Some terms are properly localized:
- "RNCP" is kept in both versions (correct, as it is a proper noun)
- "Qualiopi" is kept in both versions (correct)
- "Ingenierie pedagogique" becomes "Instructional Design" (correct)

But others are not adapted:
- "Vulgarisation" becomes "education" in some places and "breaking down" in others. The concept of "vulgarisation scientifique" does not have a clean English equivalent. "Making complex topics accessible" works better as a consistent translation.
- "Retour d'experience" is sometimes "case study" and sometimes "field report." Pick one.
- "Disponible en consulting et freelance" is identical in both versions. The EN market distinguishes between "consulting" and "freelance" differently than the FR market. Consider "Available for consulting engagements and contract work."

**Issue 3: Missing cultural context.**
- "Baccalaureat professionnel" is translated as "Professional Baccalaureate" -- which means nothing to a non-French reader. Consider "Vocational Diploma (equivalent to NVQ Level 3)" or simply "Vocational Diploma in Electrical Engineering."
- "DPO certifie CNIL" is translated as "CNIL-certified DPO" -- the CNIL acronym is not recognized internationally. Consider "Data Protection Officer (certified by France's national data authority)."

### Specific Translation Fixes Needed

| FR | Current EN | Recommended EN |
|----|-----------|----------------|
| changer les choses | change the world | change things for the better |
| je vulgarise tout ce qui me tombe sous la main | I break down anything I get my hands on | I make complex topics accessible across any format |
| Baccalaureat professionnel | Professional Baccalaureate | Vocational Diploma |
| DPO certifie CNIL | CNIL-certified DPO | Certified Data Protection Officer (CNIL, France) |
| Multipotentialiste assume | Proud multipotentialist | Proud generalist -- always 5 projects running |
| Ils parlent de moi | What they say | What colleagues say |

## 9. EMOTIONAL RESONANCE vs. PROFESSIONALISM BALANCE -- Score: 7/10

### Analysis

Pierre's content strikes a good balance overall, leaning appropriately toward professionalism while maintaining a human voice. The tone is consistent with the stated preference for "anti-storytelling, direct/expert tone."

**What works:**
- The process descriptions are personal ("J'ecoute, j'audite, je comprends le vrai probleme") without being sentimental
- Testimonials provide emotional proof through third-party voices rather than self-promotion
- The fun facts section (SpaceX, jewelry, running) adds humanity without undermining authority
- Experience descriptions are honest about context ("Recrute sur un poste administratif, mon N+1 a vu en moi un expert tech") -- this is self-aware, not self-deprecating

**What needs adjustment:**

**Too emotional / off-brand:**
- "Je crois que l'education peut changer les choses" as the headline -- this is a belief statement, not a capability statement. It tilts the balance toward idealism when the rest of the site leans practitioner.
- The Gradly "story" section starts well but the phrase "J'ai commence par un prototype pour mon propre usage. Puis les collegues l'ont voulu. Puis d'autres campus." has a narrative cadence that borders on the LinkedIn storytelling style Pierre explicitly rejects.
- Blog article 6 ("Quand l'objectif s'ecroule...") about a failed half-marathon is the only piece that leans personal/motivational. It is well-written but feels out of place in the overall content strategy.

**Not emotional enough:**
- The hiring page is too transactional. A recruiter or hiring manager wants to understand Pierre as a person who would fit their team, not just a list of skills and metrics. One paragraph of "what colleagues say about working with me" (in Pierre's own words, not just testimonials) would help.
- The "hello" / "bonjour" page is surprisingly thin. For a page meant to introduce Pierre in 30 seconds, it just repeats the homepage content with a photo and fun facts. It should have a unique, concise narrative that the homepage does not have.

## 10. ABOUT/BIO SECTION EFFECTIVENESS -- Score: 5/10

### Current State

The "Bonjour" / "Hello" page contains:
- Photo
- "Enchante!" / "Nice to meet you!" headline
- "Pierre Touzet en 30 secondes" subtitle
- The same tagline and description as the homepage
- Service tags (same as homepage)
- Fun facts (same as homepage footer)
- CTAs (email, LinkedIn, portfolio link)

### Analysis

**This page adds almost no new information.** A visitor who has already seen the homepage will find nothing new on the "bonjour" page. The subtitle promises "Pierre Touzet in 30 seconds" but delivers exactly the same content.

**The page lacks a narrative.** Unlike every other page on the site, the about page should tell a story -- even for someone with an anti-storytelling preference. The story does not need to be emotional or dramatic. It can be factual and direct:

"Bac pro electrotechnique. 11 ans en PME. Un pivot vers la pedagogie. Aujourd'hui je pilote les programmes de 6 campus et j'ai cree un outil utilise par des dizaines d'equipes. Mon moteur : rendre le complexe accessible."

That is 30 seconds. It is not storytelling. It is a career summary that feels like a person, not a LinkedIn profile.

### Recommended Rewrite for the Bio Section

**FR (new, unique content for the "bonjour" page):**

"Bac pro electrotechnique en poche, j'ai passe 11 ans dans une PME de 2 personnes ou je faisais tout : IT, achats, gestion. Un jour, j'ai eu envie de transmettre.

J'ai rejoint un reseau de 28 campus comme coordinateur. En moins de 2 ans, j'avais deploye des outils numeriques sur l'ensemble du reseau -- 100% d'adoption. Puis on m'a confie les programmes d'une ecole de tourisme en pleine croissance : 6 campus, 11 personnes sur le terrain, 400 apprenants.

En parallele, j'ai cree Gradly (temps admin divise par 3), anime un podcast quotidien, une emission Twitch hebdo, une chaine YouTube, une newsletter. Parce que le meilleur moyen de comprendre la pedagogie, c'est de la pratiquer partout.

Aujourd'hui, je suis disponible en consulting et en freelance. Si vous cherchez quelqu'un qui comprend a la fois les enjeux pedagogiques et les outils, on devrait parler."

**EN equivalent:**

"I started with a vocational diploma in electrical engineering and spent 11 years in a 2-person company doing everything: IT, procurement, operations. Then I wanted to teach.

I joined a 28-campus education network as a coordinator. In under 2 years, I had deployed digital tools across every campus -- 100% adoption. Then I was trusted with the programs of a fast-growing tourism school: 6 campuses, 11 coordinators, 400 learners.

Along the way, I built Gradly (admin time divided by 3), hosted a daily tech podcast, a weekly Twitch show, a YouTube channel, a newsletter. Because the best way to understand education is to practice it everywhere.

Today I am available for consulting and contract work. If you need someone who understands both the pedagogy and the tools, we should talk."

### 1. Rewrite the headline (Impact: 9 | Effort: 1)
Replace "Je crois que l'education peut changer les choses" with a capability-first statement. The current subheadline ("Je rends le complexe accessible") is stronger than the headline. Promote it.

### 2. Rewrite the "Bonjour" / "Hello" page bio (Impact: 7 | Effort: 2)
Add a unique 4-paragraph career narrative that does not duplicate the homepage. Use the rewrite provided in section 10 above.

### 3. Upgrade CTAs across all pages (Impact: 8 | Effort: 2)
Replace generic "Contact me" / "Let's talk" with context-specific CTAs. The consulting page CTA should be different from the hiring page CTA.

### 4. Fix the EN headline and key translation inconsistencies (Impact: 6 | Effort: 1)
Align "change the world" back to "change things" (or better, eliminate the belief statement entirely). Fix "Professional Baccalaureate," "CNIL-certified DPO," and the casual tone of "I break down anything I get my hands on."

### 5. Add "ingenieur pedagogique" keyword to hero, meta, and consulting page (Impact: 7 | Effort: 1)
This is Pierre's core professional identity. It should appear explicitly in the homepage description, the meta title, and the consulting page subtitle.
